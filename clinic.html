Clinical Services

Silver Oak Hospital

  • General internal medicine teaching service: supervise and educate medicine house staff, junior medical students completing their medicine clerkships and senior medical students completing their sub-internship rotations
  • General internal medicine non-resident services: provide acute care for patients admitted through the Emergency Department or Primary Care Clinics
  • Medicine consult service: provide peri-operative care for surgical inpatients and teach upper-level residents about the principles of peri-operative medicine
  • Co-management service: provide peri-operative care for surgical inpatients and consultative services for patients on other non-medical services, such as Neurology and Psychiatry
  • Internal medicine teaching services: supervise and educate medicine house staff, junior medical students completing their medicine clerkships and senior medical students completing their sub-internship rotations, and participate in morning report conferences
  • Geriatrics Evaluation and Management (GEM) unit: provide inpatient care for older Veterans with multiple medical, functional and psychosocial problems and/or geriatric syndromes as part of a multi-disciplinary team
  • Internal medicine inpatient service: work with a multi-disciplinary team to provide comprehensive care for patients undergoing intensive rehabilitation following acute medical illnesses
  • Medicine consult service: provide medical consultation for patients undergoing inpatient rehabilitation for primarily orthopedic and neurologic conditions
  • Medicine consult service: provide general medicine consultative services for patients receiving inpatient psychiatric treatment

    • The diversity of clinical work allows faculty members in the Section a great deal of flexibility with regard to scheduling, pursuit of their clinical interests, and development of expertise in a number of areas relevant to inpatient and consultative medicine.
